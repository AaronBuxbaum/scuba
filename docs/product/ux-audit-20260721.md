# UX audit and work plan — 2026-07-21

> A designer's pass over the product as it exists **today**: every finding below was verified
> against the running app (authenticated screenshots, a real scripted booking) and the shipped code,
> with file:line evidence. It supersedes the "unbuilt limbs" reading in
> [product-space-investigation.md](product-space-investigation.md) — the three differentiator
> surfaces (blocker queue, one-screen check-in, `/ready` page) **now exist** and are structurally
> good. The next arc is not new surfaces; it is making the existing ones *act*.
>
> Work items are specced for an agent with zero context: problem → evidence → design spec →
> acceptance criteria. Items marked **safety** require a `dive-domain-expert` review before merge.

## Where the product actually is

The daily loop is built and the bones are excellent: Today is a genuinely opinionated work queue
(`src/lib/today.ts` ranks by boat time and fix latency), check-in is properly boat-moded (44px+
targets, offline indicator, server-side readiness re-check on board), the manifest is a serious
multi-checkpoint safety document with undo and print, and the `/ready/[token]` page speaks honest,
plain language. The marketing site is polished and the booking spine is seat-safe.

The audit found one repeated failure pattern across every surface, plus four localized problems:

1. **Buttons lie, pages only point.** Queue rows labeled "Send waiver" / "Nudge waiver" navigate
   two screens away from the actual send. The diver readiness page names the diver's next step and
   offers no way to take it. The waitlist promises contact that no mechanism delivers. The product
   *describes* work instead of *doing* it — that is the opposite of "less staff coordination work."
2. **The one page divers convert on buries its own form.** The booking form and the post-booking
   confirmation both render at the bottom of a ~6,000px dive-site content page.
3. **The boat loop does a network round-trip per tap** — on the two surfaces used with wet hands
   on marina Wi-Fi.
4. **A safety field on the manifest is never collected.** Emergency contact prints "Not on file"
   for every real diver, because no diver-facing flow asks for it.
5. **Nothing is findable by name.** No global search, no command palette; "pull up Priya" means
   navigating to the right list first.

The queue below is ordered by leverage against the north star (less coordination work · more diver
confidence · safer departure). P0 items are independent and sized for parallel agents; each states
its owned paths.

## P0 — make the existing surfaces act

### WP-1 · One-tap waiver send from Today and Blockers (verb honesty)

**Problem.** Blocker-queue and Today rows carry imperative labels — "Send waiver", "Nudge waiver",
"Send waivers" — but every one is a `Link` to the trip roster, where the real send lives two taps
deeper (`src/lib/blockers.ts:22-36`, action labels in `src/lib/today.ts:118-233`, actual send in
`src/app/shop/[shopSlug]/trips/[id]/actions.ts:222-277`). On the demo shop's blockers page this
renders ~20 near-identical "Send waiver" rows that all merely navigate. A user who taps "Nudge
waiver" reasonably believes a nudge went out. This is the single highest-friction spot in the
staff loop.

**Design spec.**
- Convert the waiver rows' primary action on `/shop/[shopSlug]/blockers` (and the corresponding
  Today queue rows) into a **form button posting a server action** that performs the same work as
  `issueWaiverAction`: issue/reissue the request and send the email when the person has an email
  and an app origin exists. Extract the shared logic out of `trips/[id]/actions.ts` into a helper
  both routes call (e.g. `src/db/waiver-issue.ts` or a colocated `blockers/actions.ts` importing a
  shared lib) — do not duplicate the transaction.
- On success, show the existing `ShopNotice` pattern: "Waiver sent to Diego Alvarez." When email
  is not configured or the person has no email, the action must **not silently no-op**: return the
  private fallback link state exactly like the roster does (`?notice=waiver-link`) and show it in
  the notice panel with a copy button.
- Add a **batch action per trip group**: when N divers on one trip share the missing-waiver
  blocker, the group header gets "Send all N waivers" (one action, N sends, one summary notice:
  "9 sent · 2 have no email — links below"). The blockers page already groups by trip
  (`blockers/page.tsx:44-81`).
- Labels stay verbs only where the tap performs the verb. Rows whose fix genuinely lives elsewhere
  (add a card, take payment) keep navigation but get pointing labels: "Open Diego's record",
  "Open roster".
- Keep every button ≥44px; after a send, the row updates to the awaiting state ("Waiver sent ·
  just now") rather than disappearing without explanation.

**Acceptance.** Tapping "Send waiver" on blockers sends (or surfaces the fallback link) without
leaving the page; batch send works per trip; no imperative label anywhere in Today/Blockers merely
navigates; unit tests cover send, no-email, not-configured, and batch paths; e2e covers one
blockers-page send. **Owned paths:** `src/app/shop/[shopSlug]/blockers/**`, `src/lib/blockers.ts`,
`src/components/today/**`, one shared helper file. Effort M.

### WP-2 · Make `/ready` transactional, and deliver it

**Problem.** The no-login readiness page is 100% read-only. It tells the diver "Next: Sign your
waiver" but has no way to sign; "bring your card" but nothing to upload; "there's a balance" but
no pay button (`src/app/ready/[token]/page.tsx:20-63`, `src/lib/readiness-summary.ts:37-117`).
Worse, its waiver copy says "Your waiver link is on its way from the shop" — in a default
deployment with no `RESEND_API_KEY`, **nothing is on its way**, and staff must click send
manually. Verified live: a fresh scripted booking's ready page dead-ends exactly this way. And the
readiness link itself is never emailed — it appears only on the confirmation screen
(`BookingConfirmation.tsx:136-141`); a diver who closes the tab loses the page forever
(`src/lib/notifications/index.ts:33-38` has only two notification kinds).

**Design spec.**
- **Sign from the page:** when an issued, unexpired waiver link exists for the booking, render the
  action item as a real button "Sign your waiver" linking to `/waivers/[token]`. When none has
  been issued, change copy to the honest state: "The shop hasn't sent your waiver yet — you can
  ask for it when you arrive, or reach them below." Never claim an email is coming unless a
  delivery attempt was recorded (`notification_delivery_attempts` is queryable).
- **Rental fit from the page:** reuse the existing `RentalFitForm` the booking confirmation
  already embeds (`BookingConfirmation.tsx:105-158`) under the "setup" checklist item. Same
  component, same server action, token-authorized.
- **Emergency contact from the page:** see WP-4; the field set lands here too.
- **Pay from the page:** when the shop can charge (same guard as
  `schedule/[id]/page.tsx:99-106`) and the booking is unpaid, render "Pay for this trip" starting
  the same Stripe Checkout session flow. Abandonment already degrades safely.
- **Contact that works on a phone:** replace the text-only "Reach out to {shop.name}"
  (`page.tsx:168-170`) with `tel:` and `mailto:` links when the shop has phone/contact email.
- **Deliver the link:** fold the readiness URL into the booking-confirmation email template
  (`src/lib/notifications/email.ts:40-52`) — one sentence, one link: "Track what's left before
  you sail". This avoids a new notification kind and reuses idempotency. Do **not** create a
  separate email.
- The earned moment when everything clears stays exactly as is.

**Acceptance.** From a booking with an issued waiver link, the ready page signs, saves fit, and
(Stripe-connected) pays without any email dependency; no copy promises an email unless one was
recorded as sent; the confirmation email contains the ready link; token-scoped writes are
authorized server-side against the booking, with adversarial tests (wrong token, cancelled
booking, expired waiver link). **Safety:** payment and waiver-state copy — request
`dive-domain-expert` review. **Owned paths:** `src/app/ready/**`, `src/lib/readiness-summary.ts`,
`src/lib/notifications/email.ts`. Effort M–L.

### WP-3 · Put booking and confirmation above the content, not below it

**Problem.** The public trip page renders dive briefings, a landmarks feature, an 11-species field
guide, and a "diver moment" photo **above** the "Grab a spot" form; on a phone the form starts
~5,500px down. After booking, the confirmation ("You're on the boat, Avery!") renders in the same
bottom slot of the same long page — the diver's reward for paying is a screen they must scroll a
creature gallery to find. Verified by full-page screenshots of
`/shop/blue-mantis/schedule/[id]` before and after a real booking. The sticky "Book · N left" bar
(`page.tsx:135-142`) mitigates discovery of the form but not the buried confirmation, and the
sub-minute-booking promise (vision: "a diver finishes in under a minute") is working against the
page structure.

**Design spec.**
- **Reorder the page:** hero (trip name, date, price, spots left) → booking card → conditions/
  pack-with-confidence essentials → dive briefings and field-guide content last. The content is
  good pre-trip reading; it must not stand between the diver and the seat.
- **Confirmation takes over the top.** When `?booking=` resolves, render the confirmation as the
  first section under the hero (or scroll-anchor to it on load); the content library stays below
  for the now-committed diver to browse. The confirmation already contains the right things
  (what's-next, readiness link, rental fit) — placement is the whole fix.
- **Sold-out and past states** move up identically (they already exist as components —
  `TripFullSection`, `TripSailedNotice`).
- Keep the mobile sticky CTA; it should scroll to the booking card, which is now one flick away
  instead of fifteen.

**Acceptance.** On a 390px viewport the booking form's first field is visible within one viewport
scroll of the hero; post-booking, the confirmation headline is visible without scrolling past any
field-guide content; e2e asserts the confirmation is above the briefings section in DOM order.
**Owned paths:** `src/app/shop/[shopSlug]/schedule/[id]/**`. Effort S–M.

### WP-4 · Collect emergency contact from the diver (**safety**)

**Problem.** `people.emergencyContactName/Phone` print on the boat manifest — "Not on file" when
missing (`manifest/page.tsx:371-374`) — but **no diver-facing flow ever asks for them**. Only
seed data populates them; real bookings will sail with a manifest full of "Not on file"
(`src/db/schema.ts:75-76`, onboarding even writes the placeholder literal "On file",
`src/app/actions/onboard.ts:95-96`). A safety document that structurally can't be filled is the
kind of quiet failure this product exists to prevent.

**Design spec.**
- **Primary capture in the waiver flow:** after the medical questionnaire, before signature, add
  two fields — contact name, contact phone (`type="tel"`, `autoComplete` name/tel) — stored on the
  person at completion. The waiver is the one flow nearly every diver completes and already writes
  person-linked evidence. Optional at signing (do not block a legally distinct document on it) but
  visually presented as part of "before you sail".
- **Secondary capture on `/ready`:** a checklist item "Emergency contact" — done ✓ when on file,
  "Your turn" with the same two fields inline when missing. This composes with WP-2.
- **Staff visibility:** missing emergency contact on a booked diver joins the Today/blockers
  pipeline as a low-severity, dock-settleable item (it should rank below waiver/cert per the
  existing severity model in `today.ts:45-62`) — but only for boats in the next 3 days, to avoid
  queue noise.
- Never block boarding on it (it is not a readiness blocker today; keep it that way unless the
  domain review says otherwise).

**Acceptance.** A diver completing a waiver with contact fields sees them on their ready page;
manifest shows the values; queue surfaces missing contacts ≤3 days out; regression tests for
person-write on waiver completion and for the manifest rendering. **Safety:** request
`dive-domain-expert` review on placement, wording, and whether it may ever block. **Owned
paths:** `src/app/waivers/[token]/**`, `src/lib/medical.ts` (only if the questionnaire schema
carries it), `src/db/today.ts`, `src/app/ready/**` (coordinate with WP-2 if parallel). Effort M.

### WP-5 · Forgiving booking form

**Problem.** Email is the diver's only lifeline (confirmation, waiver, readiness link) and the
form does nothing to protect it: no `autoComplete` attributes, no typo detection
(`BookingPartyFields.tsx:39-49`). The schema defines `phone` and `buddyPreference` that the form
never renders and the action never parses (`schedule/[id]/actions.ts:25-26,48-52`) — so a shop
cannot phone a diver whose email bounced. Any validation failure redirects to `?error=invalid`
and **discards everything typed** (`actions.ts:53-55`) — six divers' names, gone.

**Design spec.**
- Add `autoComplete="name"` / `autoComplete="email"` (and `inputMode="email"`) to party fields.
- Add an **optional phone field** for the lead booker only (`type="tel"`,
  `autoComplete="tel"`), parsed into the existing schema field. Label: "Phone (if the crew needs
  to reach you)". Remove `buddyPreference` from the schema — it's a dead field; deleting beats
  hedging.
- **Email typo nudge, not gate:** client-side, after blur, check the domain against a short
  common-domain list (gmail/hotmail/yahoo/outlook/icloud + obvious TLD slips) and offer "Did you
  mean avery@gmail.com?" as a one-tap correction chip. Never block submission on it.
- **Inline validation that keeps input:** convert the booking form to `useActionState` so a
  failed parse re-renders with typed values and a per-field message, instead of the redirect.
  Native `required` stays as the first line.

**Acceptance.** Autofill fills name+email on iOS/Android profiles; a typo'd `gmial.com` shows a
suggestion chip; a validation failure preserves all party fields; phone persists to the booking
person; unit tests for the typo matcher (it must not false-positive on legitimate domains like
`gmx.com`). **Owned paths:** `src/components/BookingPartyFields.tsx`,
`src/app/shop/[shopSlug]/schedule/[id]/actions.ts` + booking sections. Effort S–M.

## P1 — speed, consistency, findability

### WP-6 · Optimistic boarding with a visible pending state (**safety**)

**Problem.** Check-in and manifest do a full server action + redirect round-trip per tap
(`check-in/page.tsx:86-104`, `manifest/page.tsx:84-104`); no `useOptimistic` exists anywhere in
the repo. These are exactly the surfaces used on marina Wi-Fi. The offline manifest machinery
already handles queued events with idempotency and server-side re-check — the *live* path is the
one that feels slow.

**Design spec.**
- On check-in, make Board update the card instantly to a **pending** visual ("Boarding…", subtle,
  not the confirmed ✓), then settle to Aboard ✓ or roll back with the existing worded banner when
  the server refuses (readiness re-check must stay server-authoritative — never render the
  confirmed state before the server confirms; this is the safety line).
- Same treatment for manifest Mark boarded / not boarded.
- Keep the full-page fallback for no-JS.
- Do **not** make blocker resolution optimistic anywhere.

**Acceptance.** On a throttled connection a Board tap gives <100ms visual feedback and never shows
a confirmed ✓ before server confirmation; refusal rolls back with the explanatory banner; e2e
covers refuse-then-rollback. **Safety:** `dive-domain-expert` review — the pending/confirmed
distinction is the whole point. **Owned paths:** `trips/[id]/check-in/**`,
`trips/[id]/manifest/**` (client components + actions). Effort M.

### WP-7 · One undo model, everywhere it's safe

**Problem.** Three undo mechanics coexist (manifest re-tap toggle; notice-banner undo on booking
remove and diver delete) and two gaps: check-in has **no un-board at all** (a mis-tap forces a
context switch to the manifest), and waiver resend uses a blocking `confirm()` dialog instead of
undo. Inconsistent mental models on adjacent surfaces.

**Design spec.** Add un-board to check-in using the manifest's re-tap pattern (tap "Aboard ✓" →
clears, with the same "tap again to undo" hint). Document the rule in
`docs/design/principles.md`: *reversible mutations get undo (banner for destructive/rare, re-tap
for high-frequency toggles); irreversible or send-actions get a confirm.* Sweep Today/blockers/
roster actions to comply. **Acceptance:** un-board works on check-in without leaving the screen;
principles doc updated; no `confirm()` remains on a reversible action. **Owned paths:** check-in
page + components, principles doc. Effort S.

### WP-8 · Global search (⌘K and a nav button)

**Problem.** No global search or command palette exists anywhere (verified by grep and in the nav
shell); the only search in the product is the Divers page's submit-only `?q=` filter over an
in-memory list (`divers/page.tsx:38-46`). "Pull up a diver" — the front desk's most common task —
always starts with navigation.

**Design spec.**
- A palette opened by ⌘K / Ctrl-K and a **visible search button in `ShopNav`** (phone users have
  no keyboard; the button matters more than the shortcut). Overlay with a single input, results
  grouped: **Divers** (name/email/phone), **Trips** (title, date words like "wednesday", site
  name), **Go to** (Today, Blockers, Schedule, Divers, Waivers, Settings, plus "Check-in — {next
  departure}" as a dynamic entry).
- Server endpoint scoped to the shop doing indexed `ilike` queries capped at ~8 results per
  group; debounced 150ms. No new runtime dependency — build it with existing primitives (a
  `cmdk`-style lib would need an ADR; hand-rolling a listbox with correct combobox ARIA is fine
  here).
- Selection navigates; diver rows go to the person record, trip rows to the staff trip page.
- While here, make the Divers list filter live (it already holds all rows client-side) — same
  debounce, no submit button.

**Acceptance.** From any staff page, ⌘K or the nav button → type 3 chars of a seeded diver →
Enter lands on their record in <2s; combobox ARIA passes keyboard-only use; e2e covers open,
search, navigate. **Owned paths:** `src/components/ShopNav*`, new `src/components/search/**`, one
query module in `src/db/`. Effort M.

### WP-9 · Waitlist that recovers seats

**Problem.** The waitlist is a dead ledger: no action exists on a freed seat — staff see a
read-only numbered list (`WaitlistSection.tsx:1-34`), the diver at position 1 is never told, and
both surfaces promise "the shop will contact you" with no mechanism. The waitlist recovers zero
revenue today.

**Design spec.** Two stages; ship the first regardless of notification policy (H-09):
- **Stage 1 — manual but one-tap:** on the trip's waitlist section and on the Today freed-seat
  row, each entry gets "Email {name} an invite" (send the trip's booking link through the existing
  notification seam; falls back to a copyable prewritten message + booking link when email isn't
  configured — reuse the `CourseInquiry` composer pattern, `CourseInquiry.tsx:30-190`). Record
  invited-at on the entry and show it ("Invited 2h ago") so two staff don't double-invite. Collect
  optional phone at waitlist join (same field spec as WP-5).
- **Stage 2 (blocked on H-09):** auto-invite position 1 on cancellation with an expiring window.
  Do not build until the policy row is Chosen; leave a seam comment, not code.

**Acceptance.** Freed seat on a waitlisted trip → one tap invites and the entry shows invited
state; no-email deployments get the composer fallback; the Today freed-seat row offers the same
action; tests cover invite, double-invite guard, and fallback. **Owned paths:**
`src/db/waitlist.ts`, `trips/[id]/_components/WaitlistSection.tsx`, `src/db/today.ts` row action.
Effort M.

### WP-10 · The boat loop gets a spine

**Problem.** Prep, check-in, and manifest are reachable only from Today's departure cards and
partial cross-links; the staff **trip page itself links to none of them**, prep is a dead-end
(back-to-trip only, `prep/page.tsx:56-60`), and the nav shell offers no path back to today's boat.
A captain who wanders loses the manifest.

**Design spec.** A compact sub-nav on all four trip surfaces (trip · check-in · manifest · prep),
rendered under the trip header: `Overview · Check-in · Manifest · Prep`, current page marked,
44px targets. On days a shop has a departure, the ShopNav "Boat view" badge slot becomes a link to
today's next departure's check-in for everyone (it already computes boat context,
`ShopNavLinks.tsx:37,49-54`). **Acceptance:** every trip surface reaches the other three in one
tap; e2e asserts the sub-nav on all four. **Owned paths:** `trips/[id]/**` shared component,
`ShopNavLinks`. Effort S.

### WP-11 · Small honesty and dead-end fixes (batchable)

One agent, one pass, all S:
- **Waiver stepper lies:** the 1-2-3 progress indicator is hardcoded to step 1
  (`waivers/[token]/page.tsx:204-211`). Make it reflect actual position or delete it; a lying
  progress indicator is worse than none.
- **Waiver completion dead-ends:** the done screen links only "Return to the shop" → `/`
  (`:304-309`). Link to the booking's ready page instead ("See what's left before you sail") —
  the token knows its booking.
- **Email-failure rows on Today** only offer "Open trip" (`db/today.ts:344-345`); wire the
  existing resend action as the row's primary one-tap action (pattern from WP-1).
- **Schedule empty state is diver-voiced for staff** ("call the shop", `schedule/page.tsx:154-160`);
  branch the copy for the staff view ("No trips on the books — schedule your first departure").
- **Blockers duplicate-person hint:** a diver blocked on two boats shows as two unrelated rows
  (`blockers.ts:56-59`); annotate subsequent rows "also on {other trip}" so staff resolve once.
- **Payment truth on the roster:** since checkout-at-booking shipped (#77), the roster's manual
  payment select and Stripe-paid state coexist without explanation
  (`RosterSection.tsx:278-306`); show the payment's *source* ("Paid online · Stripe" vs "Marked
  paid at counter") on the roster line so "unpaid" is unambiguous.

**Acceptance:** each fix has a test or e2e assertion; copy follows principles §4. **Owned paths:**
scattered; coordinate via draft PR. Effort S each.

## P2 — when the above lands (all four shipped)

- **Role-aware landing.** ✅ Shipped: `roleLensFor` + the crewed-trip lens over Today — a
  captain/divemaster's board leads with (and badges) the boat they crew; an instructor's Today
  opens with "Your sessions" and student readiness. See
  [20260721-role-aware-landing](../architecture/decisions/20260721-role-aware-landing.md).
- **Scheduled reminders.** ✅ Shipped (#88, #93): pre-trip reminder cron with SMS/WhatsApp and
  email delivery; cadence in
  [20260721-scheduled-reminder-cadence](../architecture/decisions/20260721-scheduled-reminder-cadence.md).
- **List scale.** ✅ Shipped: Divers and Schedule are keyset-paginated (50/page) with server-side
  `ilike` search on the divers roster; stat tiles and the diver calendar come from bounded
  aggregate/month queries (`src/db/divers.ts`, `src/db/trips.ts`).
- **Booking-page content strategy.** ✅ Decided and shipped: folded, not invested. Landmarks, the
  creature field guide, and diver moments collapse behind one "What to look for down there" tap
  per dive briefing; the essential briefing (map, plan, conditions) stays in view. Revisit
  investing (shop-editable content) only if shops ask for it.

## What NOT to do (unchanged from the investigation, still true)

- No new pillars, integrations, or content surfaces before P0–P1 land.
- Don't build auto-notification cadences ahead of the H-09 decision — build the one-tap manual
  sends they'll later automate.
- Don't soften the fail-closed readiness line for any of this: optimistic UI (WP-6) may show
  *pending*, never *confirmed*, ahead of the server.

## Verification notes for implementing agents

- Every WP: `pnpm check` green, e2e when flows change, and **look at** changed UI — light + dark,
  phone + desktop (`node scripts/screenshot.mjs`). The blockers/Today/check-in/ready surfaces all
  render meaningfully in the seeded demo shop (`blue-mantis`, logins in
  `src/db/dev-credentials.ts`; today's departure has 9 blocked divers, which exercises WP-1's
  batch path).
- Forms use `<Field>`/`<FieldGrid>`; buttons use `buttonClass()`; semantic tokens only.
- WP-2, WP-4, WP-6 are safety-adjacent: adversarial tests (wrong/expired tokens, refused
  boarding, cancelled bookings) and `dive-domain-expert` review are part of done.
